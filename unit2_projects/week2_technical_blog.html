Let me break it down for you and make it really simple. Everything in Ruby is considered an object. Things that objects ‘know about themselves’ are called instance variables. Things that objects can ‘do’ are methods. An object is a combination of state and methods that use that state. Classes are used to construct an object. They are the blueprints for an object. After you create a Class, every time you create a Class.new, you create a new object following your class’s blueprint. Classes basically save you time and follow the very important rule of DRY.

Classes can be used in most applications. For instance, the your music! There are many data structures used to organize your music, including how often you play each song which changes with each listen but it your music app still follows the same blueprint to reorganize your music every time you buy a new song or only want to listen to your jazz music. Really simple.
